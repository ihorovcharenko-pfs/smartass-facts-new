Cow Facts

Udderly surprising facts about cows — the underrated animals that shaped human civilization.

Domestic cattle (Bos taurus) are among the most consequential animals in human history — their domestication roughly 10,000 years ago in the Near East transformed agriculture, diet, and land use across the Old World and eventually every continent. Modern cattle science has produced several verified surprises: cows have nearly 360-degree panoramic vision due to the position of their eyes, they form complex social hierarchies, and they can recognise individual human faces. A 2011 study at the University of Northampton found cattle can maintain individual friendships, showing measurable signs of stress when separated from preferred companions.

Cattle are also among the world's most economically significant animals, with an estimated global population of approximately one billion head. Their domestication fundamentally changed Neolithic human diets, enabling dairy consumption in populations that subsequently evolved lactase persistence — the ability to digest lactose into adulthood.
