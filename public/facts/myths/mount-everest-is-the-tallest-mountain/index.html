Is Mount Everest the Tallest Mountain on Earth? It Depends on the Measure

By SmartAss Facts Team · Last updated 2026-05-25 · More myths debunked →

⚡ Quick Answer

It depends on the measure. Everest is the highest above sea level (8,849 m). Mauna Kea in Hawaii is the tallest base-to-summit (10,210 m, but mostly underwater). Chimborazo in Ecuador is the point on Earth's surface furthest from its centre due to the equatorial bulge.

🔑 Key Takeaway

"Mount Everest is the tallest mountain on Earth." is a myth. The scientific consensus is clear, the primary sources are documented above, and the claim does not survive scrutiny. Read on for the full evidence.

✗ The Myth

"Mount Everest is the tallest mountain on Earth."

✓ The Reality

Everest is the highest above sea level, but "tallest" depends on the measurement. Mauna Kea is taller base-to-summit (10,210 m). Chimborazo is the furthest from Earth's centre.

Mount Everest holds the unambiguous record for the highest point above sea level — 8,849 metres, confirmed by surveys in 2020. But "tallest" is not the same as "highest above sea level," and when other measurements are applied, Everest loses to two other mountains. This is not a semantic trick; it reflects genuinely different and meaningful ways of describing what it means for a mountain to be big.

Mauna Kea: Tallest Base-to-Summit

Mauna Kea, a dormant shield volcano on the Big Island of Hawaii, rises only 4,205 metres above sea level — well short of Everest. But Mauna Kea's base sits on the floor of the Pacific Ocean at approximately 6,000 metres below sea level. Its total height from base to summit is approximately 10,210 metres — about 1,360 metres taller than Everest measured the same way. Base-to-summit height is arguably a more physically meaningful measure of a mountain's size than elevation above sea level, since it captures the actual volume and mass of the mountain structure. By this measure, Everest is not even close to the tallest.

Chimborazo: Furthest from Earth's Centre

Earth is not a perfect sphere. The planet bulges at the equator due to its rotation — the equatorial radius is about 21 km greater than the polar radius. This means that a mountain near the equator, even at lower altitude, can protrude further into space than Everest at higher latitude. Mount Chimborazo in Ecuador sits at 6,263 metres above sea level — less than three-quarters of Everest's height. But because of its near-equatorial position (1.5° south of the equator), the summit of Chimborazo is approximately 6,384 km from Earth's centre, compared to Everest's summit at approximately 6,382 km. Chimborazo's summit is thus the point on Earth's surface that is furthest from the planet's centre — the closest any natural point on Earth gets to outer space.

Why "Highest Above Sea Level" Became the Standard

Elevation above sea level became the standard mountain measurement because sea level is a consistent, globally defined baseline that allows meaningful comparison between mountains on different continents and ocean floors. It is not the only valid measurement, but it is the most practically useful for geography, navigation, and aviation. For most everyday purposes — understanding altitude's effects on the human body, flight paths, and relative geographical prominence — elevation above sea level is the most useful number. Everest's record is genuine; it is just not the only record.

The Verdict

Mount Everest is unambiguously the highest point above sea level. It is not the tallest mountain base-to-summit (Mauna Kea) and does not protrude furthest from Earth's centre (Chimborazo). Whether the claim is true depends entirely on which measurement you use, and all three measurements are valid descriptions of physical reality.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Mount Everest the tallest mountain?

It is the highest above sea level (8,849 m). Mauna Kea in Hawaii is taller base-to-summit at 10,210 m (most of it submerged). Chimborazo in Ecuador is the furthest from Earth's centre due to the equatorial bulge.

How tall is Mount Everest?

The official height of Mount Everest, confirmed by a 2020 joint Chinese-Nepali survey, is 8,848.86 metres (29,031.7 feet) above sea level, commonly rounded to 8,849 m. This is slightly higher than the previous official figure of 8,848 m established in 1954.

What is the tallest mountain on Earth from base to summit?

Mauna Kea on the Big Island of Hawaii, at approximately 10,210 metres from its base on the ocean floor to its summit. Only 4,205 metres of this sits above sea level; the remaining ~6,000 metres is below the Pacific Ocean surface.

What mountain's summit is furthest from Earth's centre?

Chimborazo in Ecuador, at 6,263 m above sea level. Due to Earth's equatorial bulge (the planet is ~21 km wider at the equator than the poles), Chimborazo's summit is approximately 6,384 km from Earth's centre — about 2 km further than Everest's summit.

Is the tallest mountain on Mars or other planets taller than Everest?

Yes. Olympus Mons on Mars stands approximately 21.9 km above the Martian datum (the equivalent of sea level on Mars), making it nearly 2.5 times the height of Everest. It is the tallest volcano and largest mountain in the solar system by height.
