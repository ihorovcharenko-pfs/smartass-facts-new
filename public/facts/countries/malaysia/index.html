Malaysia is a Southeast Asian country consisting of two non-contiguous regions: Peninsular Malaysia (bordering Thailand) and East Malaysia on the island of Borneo (sharing borders with Indonesia and Brunei). Malaysia is one of the world's largest producers of palm oil, rubber, and tin, and it has undergone rapid economic development since the 1970s. The country is notable for its biodiversity: the rainforests of Malaysian Borneo are among the oldest and most species-rich on Earth, estimated to be 130 million years old — older than the Amazon rainforest. Malaysia is home to the orangutan (Pongo pygmaeus), found only in Borneo and Sumatra.
