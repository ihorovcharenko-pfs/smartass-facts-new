Vietnam is a long, narrow country stretching approximately 1,650 km from north to south, with an S-shaped coastline bordering the South China Sea. It borders China to the north, Laos and Cambodia to the west. Vietnam has one of the fastest-growing economies in Asia and has been one of the world's major exporters of rice since the late 20th century. The country contains extraordinary natural diversity: Ha Long Bay, with its approximately 1,600 limestone karst islands, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and the Phong Nha-Kẻ Bàng cave system in central Vietnam contains the world's largest cave, Hang Sơn Đoòng, with a volume greater than any other known cave.

✓ TRUE FACT

The Vietnam War lasted nearly 20 years, ending in 1975.

U.S. involvement escalated in the 1960s, but the broader conflict spanned decades, ending with the fall of Saigon in 1975. Its global impact included massive casualties, political upheaval, and lasting regional consequences.

Vietnamese uses Latin script with added diacritics.

Vietnamese is written in a Latin-based alphabet (Quốc Ngữ) with extensive diacritics to mark vowels and tones. The writing system uses multiple marks per letter, enabling clear representation of 6 tones in standard Vietnamese.

The Cold War featured proxy wars between the U.S. and USSR in Korea and Vietnam.
