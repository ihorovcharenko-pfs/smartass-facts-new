✓ TRUE FACT

The Great Pyramid of Giza was the tallest man-made structure for over 3,800 years.

The Great Pyramid stood around 146 meters tall when built (now about 138 m due to lost casing stones). It remained the world’s tallest man-made structure for roughly 3,800+ years, an unmatched record for most of human history.

🤯 "The Great Pyramid of Giza was the tallest man-made structure for over 3,800 years — a record that treated history like a casual speed bump."

✓ TRUE FACT

Cleopatra lived closer to the Moon landing than the building of the pyramids.

Cleopatra lived around 69–30 BCE, while the Great Pyramid was built roughly ~2560 BCE—a gap of about 2,500+ years. The Moon landing was 1969 CE, about ~2,000 years after Cleopatra, so she’s closer in time to Apollo 11 than to the pyramid’s construction.

🤯 "Cleopatra lived closer to the Moon landing than the building of the pyramids. So technically, she could’ve been on Twitter."

✓ TRUE FACT

Mexico is home to the largest pyramid by volume: the Great Pyramid of Cholula.

The Great Pyramid of Cholula is considered the largest pyramid by volume, with an immense base and layered construction over centuries. It looks like a hill today because later structures and vegetation covered much of it.

✓ TRUE FACT

The Rosetta Stone helped decode Egyptian hieroglyphs.

The Rosetta Stone includes the same text in multiple scripts, enabling scholars to unlock hieroglyphs in the 1800s. It was crucial for translating ancient Egyptian writing after centuries of lost literacy.

🤯 "The Rosetta Stone helped decode Egyptian hieroglyphs. The original “learn a language” app."

✓ TRUE FACT

The first recorded vending machine dispensed holy water in ancient Egypt.

A famous early coin-operated dispenser is attributed to Hero of Alexandria (1st century CE) in Roman Egypt. It released a measured amount of holy water when a coin triggered a mechanism—basically the ancient world’s pay-per-pour system.

🤯 "The first recorded vending machine dispensed holy water in ancient Egypt. Early proof that convenience is divine."

Cats were considered sacred in ancient Egypt.

Cats were linked with the goddess Bastet and featured heavily in art and ritual life. Archaeology has uncovered thousands of cat mummies and cat-themed artifacts, reflecting long-term cultural importance.

✓ TRUE FACT

Honey never spoils. Archaeologists have found pots of honey in ancient Egyptian tombs that are over 3,000 years old and still edible.

Honey is naturally low in water content and acidic (often around pH 3–4.5), which makes it hostile to bacteria and molds. If stored sealed and dry, it can stay edible for thousands of years—though it may crystallize, which is normal.
