Indonesia is the world's largest archipelago nation, comprising over 17,000 islands spread across an area roughly the width of the continental United States. With a population exceeding 270 million, it is the fourth most populous country on Earth and the most populous Muslim-majority country. Indonesia lies along the Pacific Ring of Fire and has more active volcanoes than any other country — approximately 130 — including Krakatoa, whose 1883 eruption was one of the deadliest in recorded history. Indonesia is also one of the most linguistically diverse countries in the world, with over 700 distinct languages spoken across its islands.
