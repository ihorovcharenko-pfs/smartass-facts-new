Switzerland is a federal republic of 26 cantons with four national languages — German (spoken by approximately 63% of the population), French (23%), Italian (8%), and Romansh (less than 1%). It is one of the world's most linguistically diverse countries relative to its size. Switzerland has maintained official neutrality in international armed conflicts since 1815 and has not participated in a war since. Geneva hosts the headquarters of numerous international organisations including the Red Cross, the World Health Organization, and CERN (the European Organisation for Nuclear Research), which operates the Large Hadron Collider — the world's largest particle accelerator.

✓ TRUE FACT

The hang drum was invented in Switzerland in the year 2000.

The original Hang was developed by Swiss makers PANArt around 2000, creating a new steel, hand-played instrument with tuned tone fields. Its sound inspired many later handpan variants. (Billboard)

✓ TRUE FACT

In Switzerland, citizens can propose national referendums.

Switzerland uses frequent direct-democracy tools, including initiatives and referendums. Citizens can force votes on constitutional changes and challenge certain parliamentary decisions, making it one of the world’s most referendum-heavy systems.

✓ TRUE FACT

DNA was first discovered in 1869 by Swiss chemist Friedrich Miescher.

Miescher isolated a substance he called “nuclein” from white blood cells, which later became recognized as DNA. It took decades—and breakthroughs like the double-helix model in 1953—for DNA’s role in heredity to be fully understood.

🤯 "DNA was first discovered in 1869 by Swiss chemist Friedrich Miescher. Humanity stumbled onto its own instruction manual."
