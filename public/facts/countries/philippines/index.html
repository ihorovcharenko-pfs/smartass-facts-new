The Philippines is an archipelago of over 7,600 islands in Southeast Asia, with a coastline longer than that of the United States. It is the third-largest English-speaking country in the world. The Philippines sits at the intersection of three tectonic plates — the Pacific, Eurasian, and Philippine Sea plates — making it one of the most seismically and volcanically active regions on Earth; it has 24 active volcanoes including Mayon, renowned for its near-perfect conical shape. The country is one of the world's most biodiverse nations: it has been identified as one of 17 "megadiverse" countries, hosting approximately 5% of the world's species in just 0.25% of the world's land area.

The Philippines also has the second-longest coastline of any country in the world (after Canada), at approximately 36,289 km. The country's marine biodiversity is among the highest on Earth: the "Coral Triangle" that includes Philippine waters contains approximately 76% of the world's coral species and over 3,000 species of reef fish.
