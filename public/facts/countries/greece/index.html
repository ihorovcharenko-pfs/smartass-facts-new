Platform shoes date back to ancient Greece where actors wore them in theater.

Ancient Greek theater used elevated footwear (often linked to cothurni) to add height and stage presence. Modern platforms echo the idea: dramatic proportion and instant elevation, sometimes adding 5–15 cm or more.
