✓ TRUE FACT

The longest place name in the world is in New Zealand - Taumatawhakatangihangakoauauotamateaturipukakapikimaungahoronukupokaiwhenuakitanatahu (85 characters).

The New Zealand hill name is famous for having 85 letters and is often cited among the longest single-word place names. “Longest in the world” depends on naming rules and what counts as “official,” but it’s definitely a heavyweight champ.

✓ TRUE FACT

The world’s largest grand piano was built by a 15-year-old in New Zealand.

Adrian Mann began building the “Alexander Piano” at 15, and it measures about 5.7 meters (18 ft 9 in) long—over 2× a typical concert grand’s length. The long body allows exceptionally long bass strings and a huge soundboard. (The Guardian)

🤯 "The world’s largest grand piano was built by a 15-year-old in New Zealand. Meanwhile, homework waits."

✓ TRUE FACT

Women could vote in New Zealand starting in 1893, the first country to grant suffrage.

New Zealand became the first self-governing country to grant women the right to vote in 1893, influencing suffrage movements elsewhere. Many countries followed gradually over the next decades, often after sustained activism.

🤯 "Women could vote in New Zealand starting in 1893, the first country to grant suffrage. A global first for equality."

You can visit Hobbiton in New Zealand where Lord of the Rings was filmed.

The narrowest gap is about 82 km, and the Diomede Islands reduce the “visible gap” to a few kilometers between them—but it’s still extremely dangerous and not a casual/legal walk. Sea ice can form, but conditions and permissions make this more mythic than practical.
