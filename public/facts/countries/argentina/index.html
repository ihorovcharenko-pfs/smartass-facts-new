Argentina spans a remarkable range of physical geography — from the Andes mountain range along the western border, which includes the highest peak in the Western Hemisphere (Aconcagua, 6,961 m), to the flat agricultural Pampas in the centre, and the cold, windswept Patagonian steppe in the south. Argentina is the eighth-largest country by area and the second-largest in South America. Its capital, Buenos Aires, is one of the most populous cities in the Southern Hemisphere and a major centre of Spanish-language publishing, theatre, and cultural production.
