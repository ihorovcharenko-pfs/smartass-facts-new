Cold War Facts

Surprising facts about the Cold War — the decades-long standoff that shaped the modern world.

Cold War is a surprisingly rich topic with facts that challenge common assumptions. The Cold War featured proxy wars between the U.S. The Cold War lasted from roughly 1947 to 1991 without direct U.S.-Soviet conflict. The Non-Aligned Movement was a Cold War grouping of states not formally aligned with any bloc. The Berlin Wall fell in 1989, symbolizing the end of the Cold War.
