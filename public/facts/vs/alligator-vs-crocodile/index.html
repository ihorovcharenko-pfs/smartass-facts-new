Alligator vs Crocodile: What's the Difference?

By SmartAss Facts Team · Last updated 2026-05-23 · More comparisons →

⚡ Quick Answer

The easiest tell: when a crocodile's mouth is closed, the large fourth lower tooth is visible outside the jaw. On an alligator, that tooth fits into a socket and is hidden. Crocodiles also have narrower, V-shaped snouts; alligators have broader, U-shaped snouts. They also live in different regions — alligators primarily in freshwater in the US and China; crocodiles across tropical regions worldwide.

Alligator or crocodile? Both are large, armoured, semi-aquatic reptiles with powerful jaws and long tails, and from a safe distance they are easy to confuse. But they are distinct animals from different families — Alligatoridae and Crocodylidae respectively — with clear physical differences, different habitats, and different geographic ranges. The confusion is understandable; the distinction is knowable.

Snout and Teeth: The Fastest ID

The snout is the quickest and most reliable identifier. Alligators have a broad, rounded U-shaped snout — evolved for crushing prey like turtles and hard-shelled animals. Crocodiles have a narrower, pointed V-shaped snout suited to catching fish and quicker prey. The teeth are equally diagnostic when the animal's mouth is closed. In crocodiles, the large fourth lower tooth protrudes outside the upper jaw and is clearly visible. In alligators, the upper jaw is wider than the lower, so all lower teeth — including the fourth — are hidden when the mouth shuts. If you can count teeth from a safe distance, you're looking at a crocodile.

Habitat and Range

American alligators live almost exclusively in freshwater environments — swamps, lakes, rivers, and bayous in the southeastern United States, from North Carolina to Florida and west to Texas. The only other living alligator species, the critically endangered Chinese alligator, is found in the Yangtze River delta. Crocodiles have a much broader global range and significantly more salinity tolerance. The American crocodile inhabits parts of Florida, the Caribbean, and Central and South America. African crocodiles, Nile crocodiles, and saltwater crocodiles (the largest living reptile) range across Sub-Saharan Africa, southern Asia, and Australia. The saltwater crocodile regularly swims in marine waters and has been found hundreds of kilometres from shore.

Behaviour and Danger

Both alligators and crocodiles are apex predators and should be treated with respect. The saltwater crocodile is considered the most dangerous crocodilian to humans and is responsible for hundreds of human deaths annually in parts of Africa and Asia. American alligators, while occasionally aggressive (particularly females protecting nests), are generally more tolerant of human proximity and unprovoked attacks on humans are relatively rare. The Nile crocodile is similarly dangerous, responsible for significant human fatalities in sub-Saharan Africa. When encountering either animal in the wild, maintain distance, never feed them (which conditions them to associate humans with food), and follow local wildlife guidance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the easiest way to tell an alligator from a crocodile?

Check the lower teeth when the mouth is closed. If the large fourth lower tooth is visible, it's a crocodile. Alligators' lower teeth are hidden. Snout shape also helps: U-shaped = alligator; V-shaped = crocodile.

Are crocodiles more dangerous than alligators?

Saltwater and Nile crocodiles are responsible for far more human fatalities annually than alligators. American alligators are generally less aggressive, though all large crocodilians are potentially dangerous.

Can alligators and crocodiles interbreed?

No. They are in different families (Alligatoridae vs Crocodylidae) and cannot interbreed. Gharials form a third family, Gavialidae.

Where do alligators and crocodiles coexist?

Only in southern Florida, where American alligators and American crocodiles share some habitat. The American crocodile typically favours coastal brackish and saltwater areas; the alligator prefers freshwater.

Which is bigger — alligators or crocodiles?

The saltwater crocodile is the largest living reptile, reaching over 6m. American alligators typically reach about 4.5m. On average, crocodiles are larger.

Do alligators or crocodiles run fast on land?

Both can sprint short distances at 15–20 km/h on land, which is faster than most people expect. Neither sustains this speed; they tire quickly. Both are far faster in water.

How long do alligators and crocodiles live?

Both are long-lived. American alligators typically live 35–50 years in the wild. Crocodiles live similarly, with some species estimated to reach 70–100 years.
