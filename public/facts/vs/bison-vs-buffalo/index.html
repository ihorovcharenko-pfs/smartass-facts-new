Bison vs Buffalo: Are They the Same Animal?

By SmartAss Facts Team · Last updated 2026-05-23 · More comparisons →

⚡ Quick Answer

No — bison and buffalo are different animals. The American bison (Bison bison) is native to North America and is not a true buffalo. True buffalo are the African Cape buffalo (Syncerus caffer) and the Asian water buffalo (Bubalus bubalis). European bison (wisent) are a third species. The mix-up dates to early European settlers who applied a familiar name to an unfamiliar animal.

The American West's iconic grazing herds — the shaggy, hump-backed animals that once numbered 60 million across the Great Plains — are almost universally called 'buffalo' in popular culture. 'Home on the Range' sings of buffalo roaming; the Buffalo Bills, Buffalo Wild Wings, and Buffalo, New York all take their name from them. They are not actually buffalo. They are bison. True buffalo live in Africa and Asia. The confusion has a colourful history and is essentially impossible to eradicate from common usage, but the biological distinction is real.

Why Americans Call Bison 'Buffalo'

When European settlers arrived in North America, they encountered an unfamiliar bovine animal and applied the closest term they knew — 'buffalo' — from their acquaintance with African Cape buffalo (via trade networks) and Asian water buffalo (via accounts from Asia). The misnaming stuck and became embedded in place names (Buffalo, New York; Buffalo Lake; Buffalo Bill), vernacular, and popular culture before naturalists had the opportunity to correct it. Scientific taxonomy eventually established that American bison are distinct from true buffalo and belong to a separate genus. But common usage had already fixed 'buffalo' in the American vocabulary for this animal, and no amount of taxonomic correction has significantly displaced it.

True Buffalo: Africa and Asia

True buffalo are members of the Bovini tribe but in different genera from bison. The African Cape buffalo (Syncerus caffer) is a large, social grazing bovid found in sub-Saharan Africa, one of the 'Big Five' game animals, and considered by many hunters to be among the most dangerous animals in Africa due to its unpredictable aggression when wounded or cornered. The Asian water buffalo (Bubalus bubalis) is one of the most important domestic animals in the world — with approximately 1.5 billion working as draught animals in rice paddies and small-scale farming across South and Southeast Asia. Wild water buffalo are critically endangered, with fewer than 4,000 individuals remaining. European bison (wisent, Bison bonasus) are closely related to American bison but are a separate species; they were nearly extinct by the early 20th century and have been reintroduced through captive breeding.

The Near-Extinction and Recovery of American Bison

American bison had one of the most dramatic population collapses in conservation history. Estimated at 30–60 million in the early nineteenth century, overhunting for hides and tongues — often encouraged by US government policy as a means of undermining the Plains Indian peoples who depended on bison — reduced the population to fewer than 1,000 animals by the 1880s. Conservation efforts beginning in the late nineteenth century, led in part by President Theodore Roosevelt and the American Bison Society, gradually rebuilt the population. Today, approximately 500,000 bison exist in North America — most in managed herds on private ranches and national parks. Free-roaming, genetically diverse wild bison populations are far smaller, with Yellowstone National Park's herd of ~5,000 among the most significant.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are bison and buffalo the same animal?

No. American bison (Bison bison) are not true buffalo. True buffalo are the African Cape buffalo and Asian water buffalo, which are different genera. The name 'buffalo' for American bison is a historical misnaming by European settlers that stuck in common usage.

Where do real buffalo live?

Cape buffalo live in sub-Saharan Africa. Water buffalo are native to South and Southeast Asia (domesticated versions are found worldwide). European bison (wisent) are a separate species native to Europe.

Why were American bison nearly wiped out?

Industrial-scale hunting in the 19th century, often actively encouraged by US government policy as a means of undermining the Plains Indian peoples who depended on bison for food, shelter, and tools. The population fell from ~30–60 million to fewer than 1,000 by the 1880s.

Are bison dangerous?

Yes. Despite looking slow and docile, bison can run at up to 55 km/h and are unpredictable. More people are injured by bison in Yellowstone National Park each year than by any other animal.

Is a 'buffalo burger' actually bison?

In North America, yes — 'buffalo meat' or 'buffalo burgers' on menus refer to American bison meat. It is leaner and lower in fat than beef, with a slightly sweet, rich flavour.

What is a beefalo?

A beefalo is a fertile hybrid of American bison and domestic cattle (Bos taurus). Beefalo are raised commercially for beef with a claimed blend of bison leanness and cattle docility.

What is the conservation status of American bison?

Near threatened. The population has recovered from near-extinction (~1,000 in the 1880s) to ~500,000 today, mostly in managed herds. Genetically diverse, free-roaming wild populations are much smaller.
