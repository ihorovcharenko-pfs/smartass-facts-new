Venomous vs Poisonous: They're Not the Same Thing

By SmartAss Facts Team · Last updated 2026-05-23 · More comparisons →

⚡ Quick Answer

Venom is injected — by fangs, stingers, or spines. Poison is ingested or absorbed through contact. If an animal bites you and injects a toxin, it's venomous. If you eat or touch an animal and get sick, it's poisonous. Most 'poisonous snakes' are actually venomous — they inject toxin through fangs.

Poisonous snake. The phrase sounds correct, but biologically it isn't — almost all dangerous snakes are venomous, not poisonous. The distinction isn't pedantry; it describes a meaningful biological difference. Venom is injected. Poison is ingested or absorbed. An animal that bites you and delivers toxin is venomous. An animal whose flesh or skin is toxic to eat or touch is poisonous. A few animals are both.

The Simple Rule

If it bites you and you die, it's venomous. If you bite it and you die, it's poisonous. The mechanism of toxin delivery is the distinguishing factor. Venomous animals actively inject toxin through specialised structures — snake fangs, bee stingers, platypus spurs (males), stonefish spines, cone snail harpoons. The toxin is delivered into the target's body, bypassing normal digestive defences. Poisonous animals or plants are passively toxic — the toxin affects only something that ingests or contacts them. Poison dart frogs carry potent batrachotoxins in their skin; touching or eating them causes harm, but they don't inject anything. Interestingly, many venomous snakes can actually be eaten safely — their venom is a protein that is denatured and inactivated by stomach acid (assuming no mouth ulcers). Drinking cobra venom is harmless; being injected with it is lethal.

Animals That Are Both

A small number of animals are both venomous and poisonous — they can inject toxin and are also toxic if eaten. The blue-ringed octopus is venomous (it injects tetrodotoxin via biting) and its flesh contains tetrodotoxin, making it poisonous to eat as well. Some tiger salamander species are toxic to consume. The slow loris, a primate, has a venomous bite (combining toxin from a gland near the elbow with saliva) and is also mildly toxic if its skin secretions are consumed. Rhabdophis tigrinus, a snake, sequesters toxins from toads it eats in neck glands, making it both venomous (via bite) and poisonous (the neck glands are toxic if eaten).

Why the Distinction Matters

Beyond biological accuracy, the venomous/poisonous distinction has practical first-aid relevance. Venomous animal bites and stings require specific antivenoms — treatments targeting the injected toxin's specific mechanism. Poisoning from ingested toxins may require different treatment: activated charcoal, emetics, or specific antidotes depending on the poison. Misidentifying a venomous animal as 'poisonous' doesn't cause direct harm, but it reflects a misunderstanding of the biology that can affect how emergencies are communicated. The correct phrase in nearly all snake-bite contexts is 'venomous snake.' The phrase 'poisonous snake' is technically incorrect for virtually all snake species.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between venomous and poisonous?

Venom is injected (by fangs, stingers, or spines). Poison must be ingested or absorbed through contact. If an animal bites you to deliver toxin, it's venomous. If you eat or touch it and get sick, it's poisonous.

Are snakes venomous or poisonous?

Venomous — they inject venom through fangs. Almost no snake is poisonous in the strict sense (toxic to eat). In fact, many venomous snakes can be safely eaten because their protein-based venom is destroyed by stomach acid.

Are poison dart frogs venomous?

No — they're poisonous. Their toxins (batrachotoxins) are in the skin and are harmful if touched or eaten. They don't inject toxin. In captivity, where they don't eat the toxic insects that provide the precursor compounds, they lose their toxicity.

Can you drink snake venom?

Generally yes, if you have no mouth sores or stomach ulcers. Venom is a protein that is denatured by stomach acid and cannot cross the intact gut lining. Injecting it is lethal; drinking it bypasses the delivery mechanism.

What animal is both venomous and poisonous?

The blue-ringed octopus is both — it injects tetrodotoxin via bite (venomous) and its flesh contains tetrodotoxin (poisonous to eat). Some snake species that sequester toad toxins are also both.

Is the platypus venomous?

Male platypuses are venomous — they have hollow spurs on their hind ankles connected to venom glands, used during mating season. The venom causes extreme pain and is resistant to morphine. Female platypuses are not venomous.

What is the most venomous animal in the world?

By toxin potency, the box jellyfish (Chironex fleckeri) or the inland taipan snake (most potent venom per bite). By human fatalities, mosquitoes cause far more deaths than any venomous animal, though technically through disease transmission rather than venom.
